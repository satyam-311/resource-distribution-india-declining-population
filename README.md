# Demographic Inversion and Resource Sustainability  
### A Study on India's Hypothetical One Child Policy

## Project Overview

This project explores the possible impact of introducing a one child policy in India. Most discussions about population control focus on reducing pressure on natural resources and improving environmental sustainability.  

However, this project also studies the other side of the issue. It looks at the economic challenges that may arise from a rapidly aging population, such as a shrinking workforce, higher dependency ratios, and pressure on traditional family care systems.

The goal of this analysis is to understand whether the environmental benefits of population control would outweigh the economic consequences.

---

## Inspiration from Economic Research

The idea for this project came from studying India's demographic dividend. Economists like Jean Dreze and reports from the Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation explain that India currently benefits from having a large young population.

At the same time, several East Asian countries experienced rapid population aging after strict population control policies. This raised an interesting question.

What would happen if India experienced a similar demographic shift earlier than expected?

This project explores that question through data analysis and simulation.

## What This Project Adds

Many studies analyze population growth or resource sustainability separately. This project connects both perspectives through simulation and analysis.

The study focuses on three main areas.

### Workforce Contraction
A thirty year projection of the working age population between fifteen and sixty four.

### Fiscal Balance Pressure
An analysis of the gap between government tax revenue and the rising cost of elderly care.

### Societal Pressure
A discussion about the role of artificial intelligence and automation in supporting economic sustainability.
