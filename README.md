Demographic Inversion and Resource Sustainability: A Study on India's Hypothetical One-Child Policy
Project Overview
This project explores the dual-sided impact of a hypothetical one-child policy in India. While much of the public debate focuses on resource relief and environmental sustainability, this research dives into the hidden 'demographic tax'—the economic strain of an aging population, a shrinking labor force, and the collapse of traditional family-care models.

The Inspiration: Indian Economic Research
The inspiration for this study came from analyzing India's unique 'Demographic Dividend.' Research by economists like Jean Drèze and reports from the Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation (MOSPI) highlight India's current advantage with a young population. However, looking at the rapid aging seen in other East Asian nations provided the curiosity to ask: What if India artificially accelerated its demographic transition? This project was born from a desire to see if the sustainability gains would be worth the resulting 'Fiscal Squeeze.'

Built with Curiosity and Hivemind
I approached this project with limited formal knowledge of economic modeling but a high degree of curiosity. Using Hivemind, I was able to translate complex economic theories into functional Python simulations. This process taught me that the hardest part of data science isn't the coding—it is the domain understanding.

By focusing on the '4-2-1' problem and fiscal balance, I realized that data science becomes significantly easier when you understand the underlying statistics and the human stories behind the data. We often jump to algorithms, but we forget that understanding the data itself is where the real answers lie.

What This Project Adds
While existing studies often look at population or resources in isolation, this analysis adds a dynamic simulation of:

Workforce Contraction: A 30-year projection of the 15-64 age group.
Fiscal Balance Erosion: Quantifying the gap between tax revenue and the rising cost of elderly care.
Societal Pressure: A discussion on the necessity of AI and automation as a survival tool rather than just a luxury.
Repository Structure
resource_sustainability_per_capita_analysis.ipynb: Explores the positive environmental and resource-sharing outcomes of population control.
demographic_inversion_labor_analysis.ipynb: Explores the economic dependency crisis and the challenges of a shrinking youth population.
Conclusion
This study serves as a reminder that every policy is a trade-off. Through this curiosity-led exploration, I've learned that data science is most powerful when it is used to bridge the gap between abstract numbers and real-world economic consequences.
